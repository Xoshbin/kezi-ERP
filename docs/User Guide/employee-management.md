# Employee Management: Your Team Hub

This guide explains how to manage employee records in Jmeryar. Think of the Employee module as the central database for everyone working in your company—tracking everything from their first day to their last.

---

## What is an Employee Record?

An employee record is your digital file for each staff member. It connects a person to their job, their contract, and their pay.

**Why does this matter?**
1. **Centralized Data**: Keep personal info, contracts, and bank details in one place.
2. **Automated HR**: Linking employees to contracts allows for automatic leave and payroll calculations.
3. **Organization**: Clear reporting lines show who reports to whom.

---

## Where to Find It

Navigate to: **HR → Employees**

You can also find employee links in:
- **Payroll**: When processing salaries.
- **Leave**: When approving time off.
- **Expenses**: When reimbursing costs.

> **💡 Tip**: Use the search bar to find employees by name, ID number, or department.

---

## Creating an Employee

Let's walk through onboarding a new team member.

### Step 1: Start Fresh

Navigate to **HR → Employees → New Employee**

You'll see a form categorized into several tabs.

### Step 2: Key Information

| Section | Key Fields | Best Practice |
|---------|------------|---------------|
| **Personal** | Name, Email, Phone | Use their official legal name as it appears on ID. |
| **Job** | Department, Position, Manager | Set the manager correctly for approval workflows. |
| **Status** | Employee Type, Status | Mark new hires as "Active". |

### Step 3: Compensation & Contracts

While you create the employee record here, the detailed salary and working hours are defined in the **Employment Contract**.
*After saving the employee, don't forget to create their contract!*

### Step 4: Documents

Use the **Attachments** area to upload:
- ID Card / Passport scans
- Signed contract copies
- Resume / CV
- Educational certificates

## Understanding Employee Statuses

Keeping statuses accurate ensures payroll runs correctly.

- **Active**: Currently working and eligible for pay/leave.
- **Inactive/Terminated**: No longer working. Retained for historical records.

> **!IMPORTANT**
> When an employee leaves, do **not** delete their record. Instead, set a **Termination Date** and change status to **Terminated**. This keeps their history for reports.

## Common Scenarios

### Onboarding a New Hire
1. Create the **Employee** record with personal details.
2. Create an **Employment Contract** linked to this employee.
3. Assign them a **User** account if they need to log in to the portal.

### Promotion / Transfer
**The situation**: Sarah is moving from Sales to Marketing.
**What to do**:
1. Open Sarah's employee record.
2. Update her **Department** and **Position**.
3. Update her **Manager** to the new supervisor.
4. (Optional) Create a new contract if salary changes, or update the existing one.

### Offboarding (Termination)
**The situation**: John is leaving the company.
**What to do**:
1. Open John's record.
2. Enter the **Termination Date**.
3. Ensure all company assets are returned.
4. Process his final settlement in Payroll.

## Best Practices

### 🔒 Privacy First
Employee records contain sensitive personal data (bank accounts, national IDs). 
- Only give HR Managers access to view full details.
- Regular employees should only see their own public profile (if configured).

### 📅 Keep It Current
- Update bank details immediately if they change to avoid failed salary payments.
- Review emergency contacts annually.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I delete an employee?**
A: Generally, no. If they have ever been paid or requested leave, you cannot delete them without breaking financial records. Archive them by setting status to "Terminated" instead.

**Q: Why can't I see the employee in Payroll?**
A: Check two things:
1. Is their status **Active**?
2. Do they have an active **Employment Contract** covering the payroll period?

**Q: How do I link an employee to a login user?**
A: In the "Account Settings" or "User" field of the employee form, select their system user account. This lets them log in to the employee self-service portal.
