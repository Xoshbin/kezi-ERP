# Leave Management: Your Time-Off Hub

This guide explains how to manage employee leave requests in Jmeryar. Think of Leave Management as your central system for tracking vacations, sick days, and all other time off—ensuring fair allocation, proper approval workflows, and accurate record-keeping.

---

## What is Leave Management?

Leave Management handles the complete lifecycle of employee time-off requests—from submission to approval to balance tracking.

**Why does this matter?**
1. **Fair Allocation**: Ensures employees get their entitled leave days.
2. **Workflow Control**: Manages approval hierarchies (manager → HR).
3. **Accurate Records**: Tracks used vs. available leave for payroll and compliance.
4. **Team Planning**: Helps managers see who's out and plan coverage.

---

## Where to Find It

Navigate to: **HR → Leave Requests**

You can also access leave information from:
- **Employee Profile**: View an employee's leave history and balances.
- **Dashboard**: Quick stats on pending requests.
- **Payroll**: Leave days affect salary calculations.

> **💡 Tip**: Use the **Help** button in the top-right corner of the Leave Requests page—it opens this guide!

## Understanding Leave Types

Before requesting leave, you need to know what types are available. Leave Types are configured by HR/Admin.

### Common Leave Types

| Type | Description | Typical Days/Year | Paid? |
|------|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Annual Leave** | Vacation time | 15-30 days | Yes |
| **Sick Leave** | Medical absence | 10-15 days | Yes |
| **Maternity Leave** | For new mothers | 90-120 days | Yes/Partial |
| **Paternity Leave** | For new fathers | 5-14 days | Yes |
| **Unpaid Leave** | Additional time off | Unlimited | No |
| **Emergency Leave** | Urgent personal matters | 3-5 days | Yes |
| **Study Leave** | For education/training | Varies | Partial |
| **Bereavement Leave** | Family loss | 3-7 days | Yes |

> **!IMPORTANT**
> Each leave type has specific rules:
> - **Requires Approval**: Manager or HR must approve.
> - **Max Consecutive Days**: Some types limit continuous leave (e.g., 10 days max).
> - **Requires Documentation**: Medical certificates for sick leave, etc.
> - **Carries Forward**: Unused days roll over to next year (if configured).

## How Leave Balances Work

### Accrual Methods

Leave is typically **accrued** (earned) based on your employment contract:

- **Annual Accrual**: You get 20 days at the start of each year.
- **Monthly Accrual**: You earn 1.67 days per month (20 days ÷ 12 months).
- **Per-Pay-Period**: Some companies allocate leave with each paycheck.

### Checking Your Balance

To see your leave balance:
1. Go to your **Employee Profile** (or ask HR to check).
2. View the **Leave Balances** section.
3. You'll see:
   - **Entitled Days**: Total allowed per year.
   - **Used Days**: Days already taken.
   - **Remaining**: Entitled - Used.

**Example**:
- Annual Leave: 20 days entitled
- Used: 8 days (from Jan–June)
- Remaining: **12 days**

## Requesting Leave

Let's walk through submitting a leave request.

### Step 1: Navigate to Leave Requests

Go to **HR → Leave Requests → Create New**

### Step 2: Fill in Request Details

#### Request Information
| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **Request Number** | Auto-generated | LR-20260115-1234 |
| **Employee** | Select yourself (or the employee if you're a manager) | John Doe (EMP20241001) |
| **Leave Type** | Choose the appropriate type | Annual Leave |

#### Leave Dates
| Field | What to Enter | Notes |
|-------|---------------|-------|
| **Start Date** | First day of leave | 2026-03-01 |
| **End Date** | Last day of leave | 2026-03-05 |
| **Days Requested** | Total days (including half-days) | 5.0 days |

> **💡 Tip**: The system can auto-calculate days based on start/end dates. You can also enter **0.5** for a half-day.

#### Details
| Field | What to Enter | Required? |
|-------|---------------|-----------|
| **Reason** | Brief explanation | Yes |
| **Notes** | Additional context | No |
| **Delegate To** | Who covers your work? | No (but recommended) |
| **Delegation Notes** | Handover instructions | No |

**Example**:
- **Reason**: "Family vacation to the beach"
- **Delegate To**: Sarah Johnson (EMP20241005)
- **Delegation Notes**: "Please handle urgent customer emails. I'll brief you before I leave."

#### Attachments (If Required)
- Upload supporting documents (e.g., medical certificate for sick leave).
- Supported formats: PDF, images (JPG, PNG).
- Max size: 5 MB per file.

### Step 3: Submit

1. Review all details.
2. Click **Save** to create as draft (you can edit later).
3. Once ready, your manager/HR will see it as **Pending**.

## Understanding Request Statuses

Your leave request goes through these stages:

```
┌──────────┐      ┌───────────┐      ┌───────────┐
│ Pending  │ ──▶  │ Approved  │  or  │ Rejected  │
└──────────┘      └───────────┘      └───────────┘
     📝                ✅                  ❌
```

### 📝 Pending
- Request submitted, awaiting manager/HR approval.
- You can still **edit** or **cancel** it.
- Manager receives a notification to review.

### ✅ Approved
- Manager/HR has approved your request.
- Leave days are **deducted** from your balance.
- Calendar is updated—your team knows you're out.
- **Cannot be edited** (must cancel and resubmit if plans change).

### ❌ Rejected
- Request was declined (e.g., team coverage issues, insufficient balance).
- You'll see a **Rejection Reason** from your manager.
- No days are deducted.
- You can submit a new request for different dates.

### 🚫 Cancelled
- You or your manager cancelled the request.
- If it was approved, days are **restored** to your balance.

## Approval Workflow

### For Employees

1. **Submit Request**: Fill out the form and save.
2. **Wait for Approval**: Your manager gets notified.
3. **Check Status**: Go to **Leave Requests** to see updates.
4. **Take Leave**: Once approved, enjoy your time off!

### For Managers

When an employee requests leave:

1. **Review Request**: Go to **Leave Requests** and filter by **Pending**.
2. **Check**:
   - Does the employee have enough balance?
   - Is there adequate team coverage?
   - Are the dates reasonable?
3. **Approve or Reject**:
   - **Approve**: Click **Edit** → Change status to **Approved** → Add approval notes (optional) → Save.
   - **Reject**: Click **Edit** → Change status to **Rejected** → Enter **Rejection Reason** (required) → Save.
4. **Notify Employee**: The system may send an automatic notification.

> **!IMPORTANT**
> **Manager Best Practice**: Always provide a clear rejection reason. It helps employees understand and plan better.

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Planning a Vacation

**The situation**: You want to take a week off in July for a family trip.

**What to do**:
1. **Check Your Balance**: Make sure you have 5+ days of Annual Leave remaining.
2. **Check Team Calendar** (if available): Avoid conflicts with major projects or other team member absences.
3. **Submit Early**: Request leave at least **2 weeks in advance** (or per company policy).
4. **Delegate Work**: Assign a colleague to handle urgent tasks.
5. **Wait for Approval**: Your manager reviews and approves.
6. **Confirm**: Once approved, book your flights!

**What happens**:
- ✅ Status changes to **Approved**.
- ✅ 5 days deducted from your Annual Leave balance.
- ✅ Your absence appears on team calendars.

---

### Scenario 2: Calling In Sick

**The situation**: You wake up with the flu and can't come to work.

**What to do**:
1. **Notify Your Manager**: Send a message/email first thing in the morning.
2. **Submit Sick Leave Request**:
   - **Start Date**: Today
   - **End Date**: Estimated recovery date (e.g., 2 days)
   - **Reason**: "Flu symptoms, unable to work"
   - **Attachment**: Upload medical certificate if required by company policy.
3. **Manager Approves**: Usually fast-tracked for genuine illness.
4. **Return to Work**: Once you're better, your leave days are accounted for.

---

### Scenario 3: Emergency Family Situation

**The situation**: A family emergency requires immediate leave.

**What to do**:
1. **Contact Manager Immediately**: Call or message to explain.
2. **Submit Emergency Leave**:
   - **Leave Type**: Emergency Leave
   - **Reason**: "Family emergency, need to travel urgently"
3. **Manager Reviews**: Emergency requests often bypass strict notice periods.
4. **Provide Documentation**: If needed, submit proof later (e.g., hospital note, travel docs).

---

### Scenario 4: Cancelling Approved Leave

**The situation**: Your vacation plans changed, and you need to cancel.

**What to do**:
1. **Notify Manager**: Explain the situation.
2. **Edit Request**:
   - Open your approved leave request.
   - Change status to **Cancelled**.
   - Add a note explaining why.
3. **Days Restored**: Your leave balance is credited back.

## Half-Day Leaves

The system supports **partial days**:
- **0.5 days** = Half-day leave (e.g., doctor's appointment in the morning).
- **1.5 days** = Full day + half day.

**Example**:
- Leave on **Friday morning only**: Request 0.5 days for that date.
- Days Requested: **0.5**
- Your balance decreases by 0.5 days.

---

## Public Holidays

- Public holidays (configured by HR) **do not count** against your leave balance.
- If you request leave that includes a public holiday, the system may automatically exclude it from the total.

**Example**:
- You request leave from **Monday, March 3** to **Friday, March 7**.
- **Wednesday, March 5** is a public holiday.
- Days deducted: 4 days (Mon, Tue, Thu, Fri) instead of 5.

> **💡 Tip**: Check your company's public holiday calendar before requesting long periods of leave.

## Best Practices

### 📅 Plan Ahead
- Request leave **early** (at least 2 weeks for annual leave).
- Avoid last-minute requests unless it's an emergency.
- Check team schedules to minimize disruption.

### ✅ Be Accurate
- Double-check your dates and reason.
- Ensure you have sufficient balance before requesting.
- Upload required documents (e.g., medical certificates).

### 🤝 Communicate
- Notify your manager **before** submitting (especially for urgent leave).
- Delegate tasks to colleagues to ensure smooth coverage.
- Provide clear handover notes.

### 🔒 Respect Policy
- Follow your company's leave policy (notice periods, max consecutive days, etc.).
- Don't abuse emergency leave for non-emergencies.
- Keep your leave requests honest and transparent.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I request leave retroactively (for days already taken)?**

A: This depends on company policy. Generally, it's discouraged except for emergencies. Always request leave **in advance** when possible.

**Q: What happens if my manager doesn't approve my request in time?**

A: Follow up with your manager. If you have legitimate leave and sufficient balance, escalate to HR if needed.

**Q: Can I take more leave than I have balance?**

A: Usually no. The system should prevent over-allocation. However, you can request **Unpaid Leave** if your company allows it.

**Q: Do weekends count toward my leave days?**

A: Typically no. If you request Friday–Monday (4 calendar days), only Friday and Monday (2 working days) count.

**Q: What if I get sick during my vacation?**

A: Some companies allow you to convert vacation days to sick leave if you provide medical proof. Submit a sick leave request for those dates and cancel the corresponding vacation days.

**Q: Can my manager see my rejection reason?**

A: Yes. Rejection reasons are visible to the employee, the manager, and HR for transparency.

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: "Insufficient leave balance" error

**Solution**:
- Check your current balance.
- You may have pending requests that temporarily allocate days.
- Consider requesting **Unpaid Leave** or choosing a different leave type.

### Issue: Request stuck in "Pending" for days

**Solution**:
- Send a reminder to your manager.
- Check if you have the correct approver assigned.
- Contact HR if the delay is causing problems.

### Issue: Can't edit an approved request

**Explanation**:
- Approved requests are locked to maintain accurate records.
- To change dates: **Cancel** the approved request and submit a new one.
