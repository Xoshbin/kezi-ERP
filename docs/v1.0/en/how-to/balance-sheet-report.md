# Balance Sheet Report: Your Financial Snapshot

This guide explains how to use the **Balance Sheet** to understand your company's financial health at a specific moment in time. Whether you're reporting to investors or checking your net worth, this report tells you what you have, what you owe, and what you own.

---

## What is a Balance Sheet?

The Balance Sheet is a "snapshot" of your financial position. Unlike the Profit & Loss statement which covers a period of time (like "last month"), the Balance Sheet shows your status on a **specific day** (like "as of December 31").

It is built on the fundamental accounting equation:

> **Assets = Liabilities + Equity**

**Why does this matter?**
1. **Liquidity**: See if you have enough cash to pay your debts.
2. **Net Worth**: Understand the true value of your business.
3. **Debt Ratio**: See how much of your business is financed by debt vs. your own money.

---

## Where to Find It

Navigate to: **Accounting → Reports → Balance Sheet**

> **💡 Tip**: You can also find it in the "Reporting" section of your dashboard.

---

## How to Use the Report

### 1. Set Your "As of Date"
The Balance Sheet is always run for a specific date.
- **Default**: Today's date.
- **Common Uses**: End of month (`Jan 31`), End of Year (`Dec 31`).

### 2. Generate the Report
Click the **Generate Report** button (<heroicon-o-play class="w-4 h-4 inline"/>).

---

## Understanding the Sections

The report is divided into three main blocks.

### 1. Assets (What You Have)
Everything the business owns that has value.
- **Current Assets**: Cash, Bank Accounts, Inventory (things you can turn into cash quickly).
- **Fixed Assets**: Equipment, Vehicles, Buildings (long-term investments).

### 2. Liabilities (What You Owe)
Everything the business owes to others.
- **Current Liabilities**: Bills to pay, short-term loans, taxes due.
- **Long-Term Liabilities**: Mortgages, multi-year loans.

### 3. Equity (What You Own)
The value remaining for the owners after debts are paid.
- **Capital**: Money you put into the business.
- **Retained Earnings**: Profits from previous years kept in the business.
- **Current Year Earnings**: Profit or Loss from the current year (links to P&L).

---

## Interpreting the Numbers

### The Balancing Act
Just like the name says, it must **balance**.
- Look at **Total Assets**.
- Look at **Total Liabilities & Equity**.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Total Assets MUST equal Total Liabilities + Equity.**
>
> If Assets are `$100,000`, the combination of Claims (Liabilities) and Ownership (Equity) must also be `$100,000`.

### Retained Earnings Check
The **Current Year Earnings** line is a special link between your Balance Sheet and your Profit & Loss.
- It shows your net profit (or loss) for the year up to the "As of Date".
- If this number looks wrong, check your **Profit & Loss** report for the same period.

## Common Scenarios

### Checking for Solvency
**Scenario**: You want to know if you can pay your bills this month.
1. Run the report for **Today**.
2. Compare **Current Assets** (Cash + Accounts Receivable) vs **Current Liabilities** (Accounts Payable).
3. If Current Assets > Current Liabilities, you are in a good spot!

### Tracking Growth
**Scenario**: You want to see if your business is growing.
1. Run the report for **Last Year** (e.g., Dec 31, 2024).
2. Save it or print it.
3. Run the report for **This Year** (e.g., Dec 31, 2025).
4. Compare **Total Equity**. If it increased, your business value has grown.

---

## Best Practices

### 📅 Timing
- Run this report **after** you have finished entering all transactions for the month.
- Ensure your **Bank Reconciliation** is done first, so your cash balances are real.

### 🔍 Review
- **Negative Cash?**: Check if you missed recording a deposit or bank transfer.
- **Negative Inventory?**: You might have sold items before entering the vendor bill.
